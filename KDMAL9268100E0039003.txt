A decision tree is a machine learning algorithm that builds a tree-like model of decisions and their possible consequences. It is commonly used for classification and regression problems. Time series refers to a sequence of data points collected over time.

On the other hand, Support Vector Machines (SVM) are a type of supervised learning algorithm that can be used for classification and regression. They work by finding the optimal boundary between different classes in a high-dimensional space.

In terms of time series analysis, decision trees and SVM models can be used to predict future values based on past data. However, SVM models are generally more accurate than decision trees for time series analysis, as they can capture complex patterns and relationships between data elements.

Also, XGBoost and LSTM (Long Short Term memory) models are known for remembering data , sentiment analysis use cases where there are existing data with results showing likelihood patterns. 



The Pareto principle, also known as the 80/20 rule, states that 80% of outcomes are caused by 20% of inputs. This means that a small percentage of causes can have a large effect. The principle is based on anecdotal evidence and was developed by Italian economist Vilfredo Pareto in 1906.

What is Pareto principle ? | Definition from TechTarget
The Pareto principle, also known as the 80/20 rule, is a theory maintaining that 80 percent of the output from a given situation or system is determined by 20 percent of the input. The principle doesn't stipulate that all situations will demonstrate that precise ratio – it refers to a typical distribution.
